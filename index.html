Problem

How can we estimate the price of a souvlaki wrap based on the price of a single souvlaki stick?

Goal

Build a simple AI model that predicts wrap prices.

Linear Regression

How the Prediction Works

Linear regression finds a straight-line relationship between two things. In this project, it connects stick price and wrap price.

Alpha (α) represents the extra cost of pita and added ingredients. Shows how many times bigger the wrap price is compared to the stick price. Alpha is a ratio between two prices.

Wrap = α × Stick
Example: Kostas
Wrap Price €3.80
=
Alpha 1.73
×
Stick Price €2.20

Prediction: Wrap = 1.73 × 2.20 = 3.80

Alpha 1.73
=
Wrap €3.80
÷
Stick €2.20

Learned from data: α = 3.80 ÷ 2.20 = 1.73

Training Data

Small Real-World Dataset

Shop Stick (€) Wrap (€) Alpha (Wrap ÷ Stick)
How We Calculate Alpha (α)

We calculate alpha by averaging the values from all shops:

α = (sum of all values) ÷ number of shops

Graph Analysis

Who Follows the Model — and Who Stands Out?

The green line shows the model’s learned pricing pattern: Wrap ≈ α × Stick. The blue points are the real shops. When a shop is close to the line, its pricing matches the model well. When it sits above or below the line, it shows a different pricing choice.

Strong Match

Thanasis sits almost exactly on the model line, matching the expected pricing pattern.

Above Expectation

Kostas and Gyros Elladas are above the line, meaning they charge more than the model predicts.

Below Expectation

Lefteris and Savvas fall below the line, suggesting slightly lower pricing than expected.

Main Takeaway

Most shops follow the same basic pricing relationship, which means the model captures a real pattern.

Small gaps from the line = different shop pricing strategies

It is machine learning. The model learns from historical shop data and uses that learned pattern to explain existing prices and predict new ones.

Evaluation

Why This Works — And Where It Simplifies

Benefits

Simple model, real-world context, quick interaction, and an example of how linear regression can explain everyday prices.

Limitations

Prices vary by ingredients, portion size, neighborhood, labor costs, and brand positioning, so the model remains intentionally simplified.
